# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary — wholesale buyers and export operators:** Central market operators (mercados de abastos), food processors, sauce manufacturers, charcuterie producers, and export logistics companies in Mexico and the US. They visit the site to evaluate AFER Greens as a reliable, certified supplier for volume orders. They are not browsing; they are qualifying a vendor. They need: proof of certification, clear product specs, a direct path to contact.

**Secondary — food enthusiasts and trade-adjacent:** Chefs, specialty food buyers, and food industry professionals who discover the brand through a product or referral. They respond to provenance, story, and craft. They are not the conversion target but they are the reputation audience.

## Product Purpose

AFER Greens / Valle de Gigantes is a certified artisanal producer and exporter of specialty chiles (Chiltepín, Jalapeño, Chipotle ahumado con mezquite) and Melón Gran Torino from the Valle de Saucillo, Chihuahua, México. The operation holds Primus GFS certification across all three supply-chain links: farm, harvest crew, and packing house.

The site exists to generate qualified wholesale and export inquiries. Success is a buyer submitting a quote request or making direct contact. Everything else — the photography, the story, the certifications — is scaffolding toward that conversion.

## Brand Personality

Premium · Reliable · Expert

The brand is a specialty export house that happens to grow chiles. The reference feeling is a Chihuahuan-desert terroir estate: like a high-quality mezcal producer or a single-origin coffee exporter — deeply rooted in place, confident in process, never loud about it. Northern Mexico is not gentle; the product reflects that. The brand should feel like the people who grow it: direct, serious, proud without posturing.

Voice: declarative, specific, unhurried. The land and the certifications do the talking. No superlatives, no "world-class," no exclamation marks.

## Anti-references

- **Generic agro-commodity sites:** Stock-photo tractors, green-and-yellow palette, clip-art certification badges. Looks interchangeable with any wholesale produce vendor.
- **Hipster foodie brands:** Kraft paper textures, hand-lettered logotypes, warm Instagram-organic aesthetic. Too craft-market, too retail.
- **SaaS / tech startup:** Hero metrics on gradient backgrounds, Inter everywhere, card grids with icons and subtext. Wrong industry, wrong register entirely.
- **Government / institutional:** Heavy data tables, formal bureaucratic tone, muted grays. Kills the brand's premium positioning.

## Design Principles

1. **The north speaks for itself.** The Chihuahuan desert, the mezquite smoke, and the Primus GFS certificate are the story. Surfaces should surface facts and provenance, not amplify them with adjectives.
2. **Buyer confidence before buyer excitement.** This is a B2B sales instrument. Every design decision should reduce friction to contact and build trust. Authority is the primary emotion; beauty is the carrier.
3. **Artisanal at industrial scale.** The brand holds both at once: handcrafted process, certified volume output. Visual choices must honor the tension without resolving it into either pure craft-market softness or cold commodity efficiency.
4. **Provenance is the differentiator.** Where (Valle de Gigantes, Saucillo), how (ahumado con mezquite, cosecha artesanal), and by whom (Primus GFS certified). Every page should answer at least one of these.
5. **Restraint signals premium.** Quality communicates through what is left out. No overdesign, no filler sections, no decorative complexity that does not earn its place.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **Language:** Spanish (es-MX) primary, English-ready for export buyers
- **No specific known accommodations** beyond standard AA — honor `prefers-reduced-motion`, maintain 4.5:1 contrast ratios, ensure keyboard navigation and screen-reader compatibility throughout
